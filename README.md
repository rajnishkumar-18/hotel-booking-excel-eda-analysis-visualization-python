📊 Hotel Booking Cancellation Analysis

📌 Project Overview

In recent years, City Hotels and Resort Hotels have faced high cancellation rates, leading to revenue loss and inefficient room utilization. This project analyzes hotel booking cancellations and identifies the key factors affecting them, with the goal of providing actionable business recommendations to minimize cancellations and improve revenue generation.

🎯 Business Problem

* High cancellation rates in hotels are causing loss of revenue and underutilized rooms.

* The objective is to analyze booking cancellations and suggest strategies to reduce them.

🔍 Research Questions

What are the variables that affect hotel reservation cancellations?
How can cancellation rates be reduced?
How can pricing and promotions be optimized for better revenue?
🧪 Hypotheses

More cancellations occur when hotel prices are higher.
Longer waiting lists increase the likelihood of cancellations.
A majority of bookings come through offline and online travel agents.
📈 Analysis & Findings

* 37% of total reservations were canceled, significantly impacting revenue.

* City Hotels received more bookings than Resort Hotels.

* Pricing plays a major role in cancellations — higher rates lead to more cancellations.

* Seasonal Trends:

o August has the highest reservations (both confirmed and canceled).

o January records the most cancellations.

* Geographical Factor: Portugal recorded the highest cancellations.

* Booking Sources:

o 46% of clients booked through online travel agencies.

o 27% booked through groups.

o Only 4% booked directly with hotels.

💡 Suggestions

Revise pricing strategies — provide discounts during peak cancellation periods.
Offer weekend/holiday discounts in Resort Hotels to attract more customers.
Launch campaigns in January to reduce high cancellation rates.
Focus on Portugal by improving service quality and customer experience to reduce cancellations.
